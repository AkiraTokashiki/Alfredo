# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

This project follows a complete Contributor Covenant-style standard for a welcoming, respectful, and harassment-free community. Participation in Alfredo spaces means agreeing to the responsibilities and enforcement process below.

## Scope

This policy applies in project repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions, chat or video meetings, maintainer-run events, and private communication used for project purposes. It also applies when someone represents the project publicly. Maintainers may respond to conduct outside project spaces when it has a material effect on the safety or participation of the community.

## Expected and acceptable behavior

Community members are expected to:

- act with empathy, patience, and respect for differing experience, identity, culture, and opinion;
- use inclusive language and welcome corrections without retaliation;
- give and receive constructive technical feedback focused on the work;
- take responsibility for mistakes, repair harm, and respect boundaries;
- protect confidential information and avoid publishing another person's personal data;
- follow the project's security-reporting process instead of posting exploitable details publicly.

Examples of acceptable behavior include asking clarifying questions, disagreeing about an implementation without attacking a person, crediting prior work, and stepping away when a discussion becomes unproductive.

## Unacceptable behavior

The following are unacceptable:

- harassment, discrimination, intimidation, stalking, threats, or targeted insults;
- sexualized language or attention, unwanted contact, or sharing sexual imagery;
- deliberate deadnaming, misgendering, outing, or publication of private information;
- trolling, sustained disruption, personal attacks, dogpiling, or bad-faith moderation claims;
- doxxing, credential disclosure, malicious links, prompt-injection payloads, or other unsafe handling of sensitive material;
- retaliation against a person who reports a concern or participates in enforcement;
- violating a person's stated boundary after it has been made clear.

## Enforcement and consequences

Maintainers will make a good-faith effort to apply this policy consistently. They may investigate privately, ask participants to stop, moderate or remove content, limit participation, or ask a person to leave a space. Depending on severity and history, consequences may include a **warning**, temporary restriction or **temporary ban**, or **permanent ban** from project spaces. Maintainers may refer credible threats or illegal conduct to the appropriate authorities. They will explain the decision to the affected person when doing so does not create a safety risk.

Maintainers must recuse themselves from a report when they are directly involved or have a conflict of interest. Reports are handled confidentially as far as practical; information is shared only with people who need it to investigate, respond, or meet legal obligations.

## Reporting and appeals

To report unacceptable behavior, contact the repository maintainers through a private GitHub message or a private GitHub Security Advisory when the report includes security-sensitive material. Do not open a public issue for private allegations, personal data, or exploit details. Include links, dates, witnesses, and the behavior that created concern when it is safe to do so. You may report without revealing information that would put you at risk.

If you believe an enforcement decision was mistaken or disproportionate, appeal privately to the maintainers through the same channel, explain the specific decision and relevant context, and request review by a maintainer who was not the original decision-maker. An appeal does not pause a safety restriction. Retaliation for reporting or appealing is itself unacceptable.

## Maintainer responsibilities

Maintainers should make reporting channels discoverable, protect reporter confidentiality, document decisions, apply proportionate consequences, and communicate outcomes without exposing private details. This policy does not create a promise of response time or a guaranteed outcome.

This policy is intentionally aligned with the structure and intent of the Contributor Covenant while being maintained for this repository. See [`SECURITY.md`](SECURITY.md) for vulnerability reporting and [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](CONTRIBUTING.md) for project workflow.
